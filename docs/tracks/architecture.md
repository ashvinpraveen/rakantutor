# Sunway Architecture Challenge 2026
## Reimagining the Future of Learning Spaces with AI

### Overview
The Sunway Architecture Challenge 2026 invites Malaysian high school students to envision the Future Classroom—an inclusive, sustainable, and technology-enhanced learning environment. The competition encourages creativity, AI-assisted ideation, visual storytelling, and architectural thinking.

### Theme: Re-Designing the Classroom: Spaces for Future Learning
In a rapidly changing world, the spaces where young people learn must evolve to nurture creativity, inclusivity, and well-being. Classrooms are no longer confined to four walls; they are dynamic environments that influence how students think, feel, and collaborate. This initiative aims to cultivate design awareness among secondary school students, foster creative thinking, and introduce them to the discipline of architecture and design.

Using a typical secondary school classroom size of 9.0 m × 7.4 m (approx. 66.6 m²), reimagine the classroom as a space for future learning, informed by pedagogy, flexibility, technology, and human comfort. Participants are invited to rethink what a classroom could be in the future — a space that inspires learning, promotes well-being, and responds to sustainability and technology. They may explore questions such as:

- How can learning spaces adapt to different teaching and learning styles?
- How can design improve comfort, creativity, and inclusivity?
- What role do nature, light, materials, and technology play in shaping better classrooms?

### Objectives
- **Introduce architecture:** Expose students to spatial design fundamentals.
- **Creative thinking:** Encourage innovation, sustainability & critical design exploration.
- **AI integration:** Develop skills in AI-assisted visualisation & storytelling.
- **Engagement:** Strengthen school–university collaboration.
- **Talent development:** Identify future architecture and design students.
- **Communication:** Enhance expression through diagrams, visuals & narratives.

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Understand the Design Challenge:** Think about the user (age group, learning styles), problems with current classrooms, and sustainability.
2. **Choose an AI Tool:** DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, etc.
3. **Write Your First Prompt:** Start simple.
4. **Observe and Reflect:** Identify what works and what doesn't.
5. **Improve the Prompt:** Add details (materials, lighting, inclusivity).
6. **Explore Different Versions:** Test different ideas (quiet, high-energy, etc.).
7. **Select ONE Strong Direction:** Choose based on meaning, not just "prettiness".
8. **Create a Simple Process Diagram:** Show how your idea developed.
9. **Add Your Own Design Thinking:** Include hand sketches, diagrams, and notes.
10. **Write Your Design Story:** 200–300 words explaining the concept.

### Preliminary Submission
One A1 board (portrait) in PDF format including:
- **A. Design Concept:** 1–2 main visuals.
- **B. Spatial Idea:** At least one diagram (plan, section, zoning, or axonometric).
- **C. Design Story:** 80–120 words.
- **D. AI Process Snapshot:** 2–3 small images showing the progression.
