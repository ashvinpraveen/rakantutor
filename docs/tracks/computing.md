# Computing Track: AI for Healthcare & Community Safety

### Theme Overview: AI for Healthcare & Community Safety
In this year’s Computing Track, participants are challenged to architect the next generation of algorithms that address critical sectors in healthcare and public safety. This track focuses on the deep technical development of AI systems—from data preparation to model deployment.

### Mission
Develop a robust AI model that solves a specific technical problem in healthcare (e.g., patient monitoring, diagnostics) or community safety (e.g., automated hazard detection, emergency response optimization).

### Masterclass Information: AI Model Development
During the masterclass, you will:
1. **Model Selection:** Choose between CNNs, Transformers, or other architectures based on your problem.
2. **Data Pipeline:** Learn how to handle imbalanced datasets and perform effective data augmentation.
3. **Optimisation:** Techniques for improving model accuracy and reducing latency.

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Problem Scoping:** Identify a specific technical challenge in healthcare or safety.
2. **Data Collection:** Gather or curate a high-quality dataset.
3. **Baseline Development:** Build an initial model and evaluate its performance.
4. **Iterative Refinement:** Tune hyperparameters and explore different architectures.
5. **Deployment Simulation:** Demonstrate how the model would perform in a real-world scenario.
6. **Technical Writeup:** Document your methodology, experiments, and results.

### Deliverables
- **Codebase:** A complete, well-documented repository of your AI model.
- **Technical Report:** A 4-6 page PDF explaining your methodology and insights.
- **Video Demo:** A 3-minute video showing the model in action.

### Judging Criteria
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Performance** | 40% | Accuracy, precision, and robustness on unseen test data. |
| **Model Design** | 30% | Appropriateness and sophistication of the AI implementation. |
| **Data Strategy** | 30% | Quality of data preparation, cleaning, and augmentation. |
